AIRWAYS


Lohegaon Airport or Pune Airport is an international airport, located 15 km away from Pune city centre.

Carriers such as AirAsia India, Air India, GoAir, SpiceJet and Jet Airways provide regular flights for

Bangalore, Jaipur, Delhi, Goa, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Chennai and Nagpur.

Serving prime international destinations such as Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Frankfurt are the Air India Express, Jet Airways and Lufthansa operated by PrivatAir.

Visitors can avail taxi and local bus services from outside the airport to reach their destination.

RAILWAYS


Pune Junction railway station connects the city with all the prime Indian destinations.

Traveling to this city is what you are thinking about, then Cleartrip will guide you. Traveling to Pune is easy thanks to the railway connectivity.

The station is well-connected to a number of Indian cities. Some of the major routes include to the places of Bhusaval Jn, Howrah Jn, and Patna Jn.

Some of the popular trains traveling to Pune are Pune Brmt Pass(51451) to Mumbai operating 7 times a week, Pune Nzb Pass(51421) to Nizamabad operating 7 times a week, and Pune Mmr Pass(51401) to Manmad operating 7 times a week.

Traveling in one of these trains will be a memorable experience.

ROADWAYS



Pune enjoys excellent connectivity with neighbouring cities and towns by well-maintained network of roads.

Mumbai (140 km), Ahmadnagar (121 km), Aurangabad (215 km) and Bijapur (275 km) all are well-linked to Pune by a number of state and roadways buses.

Those driving from Mumbai need to take Mumbai-Pune Expressway route, which takes barely 2 to 3 hours to cover a distance of about 150 km.

The Mumbai Pune Expressway is India's first six-lane high-speed expressway, and it was built in 2002. Only four wheeled vehicles are allowed on it. This expressway has reduced travel time between Pune and Mumbai to a little over two hours. A ring road is planned around the city.
