" Peaceful Nanded city ! "

Nanded is one of the historical places in Marathwada region of Maharashtra State. It is situated on the north bank of Godavari river. It is famous for Sikh Gurudwaras. Nanded is a town of great antiquity. It is said that during the Puranic days, Pandavas travelled through Nanded district. Nandas ruled over Nanded through generations. The mention of Nanded is found in the Lilacharitra, a treatise written by Mahimbhatta. It gives the description of the idol of Narasimha in the town. ... Nanded was formerly known as “Nanditat” which is confirmed by the copper plate found at Vasim. Nanded District and the adjoining areas Nanded district is a district of Maharashtra state in central India. The city of Nanded is the district headquarters. Nanded District lies in the eastern portion of Marathwada region, which corresponds to Aurangabad Division of Maharashtra. The district is bounded by Nizamabad, Kamareddy, Nirmal and Adilabad districts of Telangana on the east, by Bidar District of Karnataka falls on the south, by Parbhani and Latur districts of Marathwada on the west, and Yavatmal District of Maharashtra's Vidarbha region on the north. The influence of Telangana, Karnataka and Vidarbha can be seen on the language, behaviour and general conduct of the people of Nanded.

Hazur Sahib

Timing:any time
Entry fee:none
Location:Gurudwara Road, Yatri Niwas Rd, Sharda Nagar, Hyder Bagh, Nanded, Maharashtra 431601

Hazur Sahib (Hazūrī Sāhib; lit. 'presence of the sahib/master'), also known as Takht Sachkhand Sri Hazur Abchalnagar Sahib, is one of the five takhts in Sikhism. It is located on the banks of the Godavari River at the city of Nanded in the state of Maharashtra, India. The gurdwara within the complex is known as Sach-Khand (Realm of Truth).[1] The structure is built at the place where Guru Gobind Singh left his earthly life. The inner room of the gurdwara is called the Angitha Sahib and is built over the place where Guru Gobind Singh was cremated in 1708. The gurdwara was built between 1832 and 1837 by order of Maharaja Ranjit Singh (1780–1839). Hazur Sahib marks the site where Guru Gobind Singh had his camp in 1708. The Guru held his court and congregation here and was convalescing after being attacked by two would-be assassins. One of the attackers stabbed the Guru, and was killed by him with a single stroke of his talwar (curved sword). The other was killed by his followers as he tried to escape. The Guru's wound was deep, but initially healed after being stitched by an English surgeon sent by Bahadur Shah I, who served as his doctor, and Dara Shikoh before him.[2] However the wound re-opened a few days later when the Guru was stringing a bow for one of his Sikhs and the Guru merged into the Primal (Joti Jot) after declaring the Guru Granth Sahib as his successorThe Sikhs built a room over the platform where Guru Gobind Singh would sit while holding his court and installed the Guru Granth Sahib on it. They called it Takhat Sahib. Guru Gobind Singh, while conferring Guruship on the holy Book, had himself named Nanded as "Abchalnagar" (literally "Steadfast city") after the first word of a hymn read at random on the occasion. In October 2008, the 300th-anniversary celebration of the Guruship of Guru Granth Sahib took place here. This site is now one of five Takhats which are places of primary importance to the Sikhs. The other four takhats are: Akal Takhat at Amritsar, Takhat Keshgarh Sahib at Anandpur, Takhat Patna Sahib in Bihar and Takhat Damdama Sahib in Talwandi Sabo, Bhatinda, Punjab

Mahur or Mahurgad is a town and religious place in Nanded district of Maharashtra, India.[1] Mahur is the birthplace of Hindu God Dattatreya.[2] Dattatreya parents Atri Rishi and Sati Anasuya Mata lived here. Brahmadev, Vishnudev and Lord Shiva once got a news about Anusaya Mata that there is no one more pious and pure as her. To test her piousness they arrived under the garb of asking Alm (bhiksha). There are three mountains in Mahur. The first one and most known is having Renuka Mata Temple, who is mother of the god Parshuram. Other two are called Datta Shikhar and Atri Anasuya Shikar Temple's. Mahur has a sacred temple to Renuka Mata, which is considered one of the three and half Shakti Peethas (temples) in the state. A big fair is held here every year on the occasion of Vijayadashami.[citation needed] Sahasrarjun attacked Renuka Devi while somewhere in today's Telangana, for he wanted to grab the sacred Kamdhenu cow — this cow has the divine power to fulfill wishes. When Renuka Mata refused to him that saying asking gift of your own choice by the guest visitor is uncalled. He attacked her & injured her. She dies in this & when Lord Parshuram knew about it, he went berserk. Then the elderly people pacified him & asked him to do the last rites in Mahur under Dattatreya's guidance. Then he was told by him that Renuka Mata will appear on first mountain for you to worship. This became the famous Renuka Mata temple. The "Matru Tirth" (means sacred place for Mother's worship) Place on this Mountain is the one which is where today stands a lake, is "Antyeshti Sthan" (Means place where last rites were performed).[citation needed] There are many other temples in Mahur like Rishi Jamdagni Temple, Lord Parshuram Temple, Kalika Mata Temple, Devdevshwar Temple, and also the Caves called Pandav Leni.

Nanded Fort / Nandgiri Fort (Marathi: नांदेड किल्ला / नांदगिरी किल्ला)is a fort located 130 km (81 mi) on the banks of Godavari river from Nanded city.[1] Nanded is situated 443 km (275 mi) from Pune. Nanded is connected by rail, air, and road to major cities in India. The fort is about 3 km (1.9 mi) from the Nanded Railway Station. There are records of Nanda and Satavahana had ruled Nanded. Nanded was known as Nanditat from the records of copper plate found in Vashim (Vatsagulma). The fort is said to be built by Yadavas of Devgiri . in 1318 Nanded was captured by Muhammad bin Tugalak. 1n 1358, the fort was under the rule of Muhamad Shah Bahamani.In 1398 a local Gond chief from Berar captured Nanded and Mahur.In 1428 Ahmed Shah Bahamani won the Nanded and Mahurgad. Burhan Nizam Shah of Ahmadnagar defeated Alauddin Imad Shah of Berar in 1527.In 1602 a fierce battle took place at Nanded between Malik Ambar and Khan Khanan of Moghul in which he was defeted. In 1617 the Moghul Emperor ShahJahan defeated the Ahmednagar rulers and gain control of the Fort . In 1670 Chatrapati Shivaji gained the control of Aurangabad and Berar. After the death of Shivaji , the Moghuls again gained The six- monthly revenue during Moghul period was Rs 20,68,193. Lord Siddheshwar Temple

Timing:any time
Entry fee:none
Location:Taroda Khurd, 431605, Nanded, Maharashtra

The Siddheshwar temple is a famous Hindu pilgrimage center located in Hottal, Degloor. The place is situated 105 kms away from Nanded city. Siddheshwar temple is believed to be from the Kalyani-Chalukya era. The temple is beautifully ornate with stone carvings and sculptures of Hindu idols and gods. The temple is built in the Hemadpanti style of architecture. The ancient name of Hottal was Pottal. The temple was built by Dev who was a Tehsil Officer during the Chalukya era. In the ancient times, Hottal was the capital of the Someshwar Chalukyas and as a result many monuments from this era is present in Hottal. The basic shape of the temple is a star, this is assumed to be a relation with the Hemadpanti architecture. Almost all the walls of the temple is decorated with many idols mainly shiv tandav, nritya ganesh, and goddesses. It is believed that the temple was built in 11th or 12th century. There is an inscription on the wall of this temple, which is believed to be of Raja Vikramaditya from 1120 AD. The monument was acquired by the archeology department in the year 1960. The Siddheshwar temple is one of the very popular destinations for religious pilgrimage. The believers say that the deity is very powerful and is one of the very rare. This temple also known as the golden temple due to the heavy ornamentation of its walls and pillars. The temple is not simply a monument but rather, an architectural wonder. It is one of those places where the creations of a dynasty have stood the tests of time. The temple is a rare specimen of this kind of architecture. The sculpture of the deity is carefully and oranately carved. The linga is carved with at most care and the craftsmanship is very well shown. The main idol is shiva and he is in 'nishchalabhaava' which means unmoved. The other deities includes goddess Laxmi and Ganesh. The temple was restore recently by the archeological department and many of the sculptures were nearly eroded and now all of it have been returned to its former glory. Believers come from near and far, the place is almost always filled with believers. The temple offers a peaceful ambience which cannot be compared. The ambience is deeply claming ang relaxing.
